Get Your Finances in Order
Before you start house hunting, know where you stand
▼
✓
Check your credit score
Aim for 620+ for most programs. 680+ opens more options and better rates.
✓
Pull your full credit report
Get your free report at annualcreditreport.com — check all three bureaus.
✓
Dispute any errors on your credit report
Errors drag your score down — fix them before applying.
✓
Calculate your monthly debt payments
Add up car payments, student loans, credit cards, and any other monthly obligations.
✓
Estimate your monthly gross income
Lenders look at your gross (pre-tax) income, not your take-home pay.
✓
Set a realistic monthly housing budget
A common guideline is no more than 28–31% of your gross monthly income.
✓
Start or review your savings
Even with DPA programs, you may need funds for closing costs and reserves.

Real Estate and Mortgage Questions
What is pre-approval and why do I need it?+
Pre-approval is a formal review by a lender of your income, credit, and finances to determine how much home you can borrow. Unlike a pre-qualification which is just a quick estimate, a real pre-approval requires a full credit pull and document review.

Why you need it: Most sellers will not even look at an offer without a pre-approval letter. It shows you are a serious, qualified buyer and tells you your real budget so you are not falling in love with homes you cannot afford.
What is earnest money?+
Earnest money is a good-faith deposit you pay when your offer on a home is accepted. It shows the seller you are serious. It is typically 1% of the purchase price — so on a $300,000 home that is around $3,000.

The good news: Earnest money is not an extra cost — it gets credited toward your down payment or closing costs at the end. If the deal falls through because of an inspection or financing issue you typically get it back. Your REALTOR will walk you through when and how it is paid.
How much do I need saved to buy a home?+
This depends on the loan program but here are realistic numbers for Oklahoma buyers:

Down payment: As little as $0 with USDA or VA loans. 3 to 3.5% with FHA or Conventional loans. Down payment assistance programs can cover this entirely for qualifying buyers.

Closing costs: Typically 2 to 4% of the loan amount covering lender fees, title insurance, appraisal, and other costs. Sometimes sellers contribute to these.

The bottom line: You may need much less than you think. Book a free call with Brooke to get real numbers for your specific situation.
How does down payment assistance work?+
Down payment assistance programs help qualifying buyers cover their down payment and sometimes closing costs. In Oklahoma the primary source is the Oklahoma Housing Finance Agency (OHFA) which offers several programs including the Oklahoma Dream program.

How it works: You apply through an approved lender like Brooke. If you qualify based on income limits, credit score, and loan type, the assistance is applied at closing — either as a grant you never repay or a second loan that may be forgivable after a set number of years.

Brooke works with OHFA programs every day. A free 15-minute call can tell you exactly what you qualify for.
