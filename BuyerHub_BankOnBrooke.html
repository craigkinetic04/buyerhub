Get Your Finances in Order
Before you start house hunting, know where you stand
▼
✓
Check your credit score
Aim for 620+ for most programs. 680+ opens more options and better rates.
✓
Pull your full credit report
Get your free report at annualcreditreport.com — check all three bureaus.
✓
Dispute any errors on your credit report
Errors are more common than you think and can drag your score down.
✓
Calculate your monthly debt payments
Add up car payments, student loans, credit cards, and any other monthly obligations.
✓
Estimate your monthly gross income
Lenders look at your gross (pre-tax) income, not your take-home pay.
✓
Set a realistic monthly housing budget
A common guideline is no more than 28–31% of your gross monthly income.
✓
Start or review your savings
Even with DPA programs, you may need funds for closing costs and reserves.
2
Gather Your Documents
Have these ready before you talk to a lender
▼
✓
Last 2 years of W-2s or 1099s
✓
Last 2 years of federal tax returns (all pages)
✓
Last 30 days of pay stubs
Self-employed? Gather your year-to-date profit & loss statement instead.
✓
Last 2–3 months of bank statements (all pages)
All accounts — checking, savings, investment. Every page, even blank ones.
✓
Government-issued photo ID
Driver's license or passport.
✓
Social Security number
✓
12 months of rent payment history
Some loan programs use rental history to strengthen your application.
✓
Documentation for any large bank deposits
Lenders will ask where large deposits came from — have a paper trail ready.
3
Explore Down Payment Assistance
You may qualify for programs that reduce your down payment
▼
✓
Ask your lender about OHFA programs
Oklahoma Housing Finance Agency offers programs for qualifying buyers.
✓
Check income eligibility for DPA programs
Limits vary by program and county — your lender can run this for you.
✓
Ask about the Oklahoma Dream program
OHFA's flagship DPA program for first-time buyers.
✓
Ask if you qualify for USDA, FHA, VA, or Conventional loan
Each loan type has different down payment and credit requirements.
✓
Understand forgivable vs repayable assistance
Some DPA programs are grants. Others must be repaid if you sell.
✓
Ask about first-generation homebuyer programs
Extra assistance may be available if neither parent has ever owned a home.
4
Get Pre-Approved
Pre-approval is not the same as pre-qualification
▼
✓
Contact a trusted mortgage lender
Work with someone who knows Oklahoma programs — like Brooke.
✓
Submit your full application and documents
A real pre-approval requires a full credit pull and document review.
✓
Receive your pre-approval letter
This letter tells sellers you are a serious, qualified buyer.
✓
Understand exactly how much you are approved for
Approved for does not mean you have to spend that much.
✓
Know your estimated monthly payment
Use Brooke's mortgage calculator at bankonbrooke.studio to run scenarios.
✓
Understand your interest rate and loan type
Ask your lender to explain every number before you sign anything.
5
Start Your Home Search
Now the fun part — stay disciplined
▼
✓
Partner with a trusted REALTOR®
Your REALTOR® is paid by the seller — their expertise costs you nothing.
✓
Make a must-have vs nice-to-have list
Bedrooms, location, school district, garage — know what matters most.
✓
Search within your pre-approved budget
Looking above your budget leads to disappointment. Stay in your lane.
✓
Attend showings with a critical eye
Look at roof, windows, HVAC, water heater, and foundation — not just the decor.
✓
Research neighborhoods thoroughly
Drive the area at different times of day. Check schools, commute, and amenities.
✓
Keep your finances completely stable
Do NOT open new credit cards, buy a car, or make large purchases.
6
Make an Offer & Go Under Contract
Moving fast and strategically wins homes
▼
✓
Work with your REALTOR® on offer price
Your REALTOR® will pull comps to help you offer competitively.
✓
Understand earnest money
A good-faith deposit — typically 1% of the purchase price.
✓
Include appropriate contingencies
Inspection, financing, and appraisal contingencies protect you.
✓
Review the seller's disclosures
Sellers must disclose known issues — read every word.
✓
Get the home professionally inspected
Never skip the inspection. Budget $300–$500 for a professional inspector.
✓
Negotiate repairs or credits from inspection
Your REALTOR® will guide you through this conversation.
7
Closing Time
The finish line — stay responsive
▼
✓
Respond quickly to all lender requests
A delayed document can delay your closing date.
✓
Lock your interest rate
Ask your lender when to lock and what your lock period covers.
✓
Review the Closing Disclosure carefully
You receive this 3 days before closing. Compare it to your Loan Estimate.
✓
Do a final walk-through of the home
Confirm the home is in the agreed-upon condition before you sign.
✓
Bring a cashier's check or wire closing costs
Confirm the exact amount with your title company in advance.
✓
Sign all closing documents
This typically takes 1–2 hours. Read before you sign.
✓
Get your keys! 🎉
You are officially a homeowner. Welcome home.
